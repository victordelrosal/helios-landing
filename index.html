Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need technical experience to join HELIOS?

+

Not at all. HELIOS is designed for people with little to no coding experience. If you can use Excel or PowerPoint, you're ready for HELIOS. The program is about building practical skills through hands-on projects, not complex theory.

How much time do I need to commit each week?

+

Plan for 2.5 hours for each live session plus 2-3 hours of practice time between sessions. The total commitment is approximately 5-6 hours per week over the 5-week program. Many participants find they want to spend more time as they see results.

What if I miss a live session?

+

All sessions are recorded and available within 24 hours. You'll also have access to the community forum where you can ask questions. For the best experience, we recommend attending live when possible, but the program is designed to accommodate busy professionals.

What makes HELIOS different from other AI courses?

+

HELIOS focuses on building real, useful AI tools from day one. You won't just learn theory—you'll implement working solutions during the very first session. The program is led by a practitioner who's trained Fortune 500 on the practical use of GenAI solutions.

Can my company sponsor my participation?

+

Absolutely. Many participants get their company to cover the investment as professional development. We can provide the necessary documentation and invoices for your expense report or L&D budget. For multiple seats from the same organization, contact us directly for corporate pricing.

What are the session dates and times?

+

The HELIOS masterclass runs for 5 sessions on Wednesdays: 6, 13, 20, 27 August & 3 September from 6:30 to 9:00 PM. All sessions are conducted via Zoom with interactive hands-on activities and live Q&A.
